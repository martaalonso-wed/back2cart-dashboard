Key Takeaways

  1. France dominates volume and performance. With 778 display users (79.7% of total traffic), FR is the only POS with enough volume for meaningful analysis. Its 20.8% CTR is the highest among POS with significant data.
  2. Spain shows moderate engagement at 12.0% CTR — the lowest among POS with recorded clicks. With only 108 display users and 13 CTA clickers, the sample remains small but directionally interesting. Close rate is also low at 1.9%, suggesting users who see the modal in ES are less inclined to interact.
  3. Belgium performs well at 18.2% CTR with 66 display users. Although volume is limited, the engagement pattern is closer to FR, which may reflect similar francophone user behaviour.
  4. Netherlands and Italy remain extremely low volume (10 and 14 display users respectively). NL shows a nominal 20.0% CTR but with only 2 CTA clickers — statistically meaningless. These POS need more time to accumulate actionable data.
  5. Italy still registers zero clicks. After 6 days, IT has 14 display users but 0 CTA clicks and 0 close clicks. This could indicate a tracking issue, very low cart-abandonment return traffic, or that the modal is not rendering correctly on IT. Worth investigating.
  6. Statistical significance is not yet reached for any POS except possibly FR. At ~189 total CTA clickers globally, we need to continue running the test. FR alone may reach significance within the next week if traffic remains stable.
  7. Close rates are uniformly low (4.1% global), which is positive — users who see the modal are not dismissing it at high rates. The ratio of CTA clicks to closes is roughly 4.7:1 globally.

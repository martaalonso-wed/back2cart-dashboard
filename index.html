Key takeaways

1. The feature drives real purchases. Out of 315 variant users exposed to the module, 10 ended up purchasing (3.2%). FR leads with 8 purchases, BE and NL with 1 each. Back 2 Cart doesn't just generate clicks — it converts.
2. The CTA→Checkout funnel is highly efficient. 61.5% of users who click the CTA reach checkout (32 out of 52). Conversion is consistent across POS: FR 63%, ES 67%, BE 60%. The module attracts users with real purchase intent.
3. Mobile dominates engagement. In FR, 93% of CTA clicks come from mobile (28 out of 30 users in free-form). In ES, 78% (7 out of 9). Mobile has a higher CTR and generates the vast majority of purchases (7 out of 8 in FR).
4. CTA click-through rate is consistent across major markets. FR 18.9%, BE 17.5%, NL 20%, ES 13.2%. The first three are closely aligned. ES is slightly below — this could be a market difference or a copy/localization issue.
5. Dismiss rate is very low — the module doesn't annoy users. Only 2.2% actively close the module (7 out of 315). Interesting finding: in ES, 1 out of 2 users who dismissed the module still went to checkout.
6. IT needs more volume. 16 users saw the module with 0 interactions in GA4. However, Tableau records 4 variant purchases for the same period. With such low volume in IT, GA4 doesn't yet have critical mass to reflect interactions. A tracking issue cannot be ruled out, but more data is needed.
7. Sample sizes are insufficient for statistical significance in most POS. Only FR (159 usr) approaches an evaluable size. ES (68), BE (57), NL (15) and IT (16) need more time. At least 2 weeks of data (~500+ users per POS) is recommended before making definitive decisions.
